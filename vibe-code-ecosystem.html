üìà Investment Thesis

The Picks-and-Shovels Play

In every gold rush, the shovel sellers win. Vibe coding is no different. The apps being built may come and go (69% make under $1K/month), but the infrastructure they all depend on is durable. Every vibe-coded app needs hosting (Vercel), a database (Supabase), payments (Stripe), and AI (OpenAI). These companies benefit regardless of which apps succeed.

üî¨ Methodology

Data Collection

TrustMRR Scraping: We scraped the entire TrustMRR /acquire marketplace on February 18, 2026. This yielded 739 startup listings with verified MRR, category, asking price, and tech stack tags. TrustMRR verifies revenue through Stripe/payment processor integration ‚Äî these are real numbers, not self-reported.

HTTP Header Probing: For the top 36 sites by MRR, we performed active HTTP probing ‚Äî checking response headers (x-powered-by, server, x-vercel-id, cf-ray), HTML source (framework markers like _next/, __nuxt, wp-content, react-root), and embedded script tags (Stripe, Clerk, PostHog, Google Analytics). Each detection was logged with its signal source.

LLM Analysis: We brainstormed 100 "vibe-codeable" app ideas across 10 categories and asked 3 AI models (Gemini 2.0 Flash, Qwen 2.5 Coder 32B, Claude) to recommend the ideal tech stack for each ‚Äî framework, database, hosting, APIs, and AI model. This produced 300+ individual recommendations.

Limitations & Caveats

  • Survivorship bias: TrustMRR only lists startups that want to sell or showcase. Many successful vibe-coded apps aren't listed.
  • Detection limits: HTTP probing catches ~60-70% of tech stacks. SPAs behind CDNs are harder to fingerprint. Many sites returned "unknown" for framework.
  • LLM training data cutoff: AI recommendations reflect training data, not real-time market conditions. Qwen recommended Heroku (whose free tier died in 2022).
  • Revenue != profit: $1.3B in verified revenue doesn't mean $1.3B in profit. Most indie SaaS operates at thin margins.
  • Correlation ‚â† causation: Next.js being common in high-revenue startups doesn't mean Next.js causes high revenue.
