## AI Models & Releases

**🚨 LiteLLM Supply Chain Attack (Major Security Event)**

The biggest AI infrastructure story today: LiteLLM (97M PyPI downloads/month) was hit with a supply chain attack. Version 1.82.8 contained malicious code that exfiltrated SSH keys, AWS/GCP/Azure creds, Kubernetes configs, crypto wallets, and more. The poisoned version was up for <1 hour before discovery — found because a Cursor MCP plugin user's machine crashed from RAM exhaustion (the attacker's bug led to detection).

As Karpathy noted in his thread: *"Supply chain attacks like this are basically the scariest thing imaginable in modern software."* Any project depending on litellm (including dspy) was potentially affected. This is a wake-up call for the entire AI tooling ecosystem.

**Karpathy on LLM Memory:** Interesting observation that all LLMs suffer from "memory distraction" — a single question from months ago keeps resurfacing as an assumed deep interest. He theorizes models develop a bias during training to use everything in context, then overfit to RAG'd memories at inference time.
