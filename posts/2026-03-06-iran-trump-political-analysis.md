## Trump's Four Exit Strategies

### 1. "Mission Accomplished" — Declare Victory, Leave (50% probability)

**The play:** Khamenei's dead, nuclear sites damaged, regime decapitated. Declare "we destroyed Iran's nuclear capability and eliminated the world's #1 state sponsor of terror." Pull back, let Israel handle mop-up.

Trump already laid the groundwork with his "4-5 weeks" timeline. This would put the exit around early April — leaving 7 months to pivot to domestic wins before midterms.

**The problem:** Iran hasn't stopped fighting. The Strait of Hormuz is still contested. Can you declare victory while tankers are being threatened?

### 2. Negotiate a "Trump Deal" (25% probability)

**The play:** Former CIA Director Petraeus has floated a "Venezuela model" — a moderate figure from Iran's existing structure emerges and seeks to "get along" with the U.S. Trump loves deals. A "Trump Peace Accord" is better branding than a war.

**The problem:** Who do you negotiate with? The leadership has been systematically eliminated. The IRGC holds real power but has no diplomatic apparatus. This takes time Trump may not have.

### 3. Full Escalation — Regime Change (15% probability)

**The play:** Go all-in. Topple the regime completely. Rally-around-the-flag effect temporarily boosts approval.

**The problem:** This is Iraq 2.0. Ground troops would shatter what's left of the anti-war MAGA coalition. The military is already stretched. No appetite domestically for another 20-year occupation.

### 4. Get Bogged Down — No Exit (10% probability)

**The play:** There is no play. The Strait stays contested, oil stays elevated, casualties mount, and the conflict drags.

**The problem:** Everything. This is the nightmare scenario. Gas at $5+, markets tanking, body bags on the news. Midterms become a Democratic tsunami.
