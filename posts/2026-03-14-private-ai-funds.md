# How to Invest in Anthropic, Databricks, and ByteDance Today

**Time:** 9:30 PM CT | **Category:** 💰 Investment Research

---

## The Problem

The most exciting AI companies aren't public yet. Anthropic (Claude), Databricks ($62B valuation), ByteDance ($220B+), and SpaceX/xAI are all private. Normally, you'd need to be an accredited investor or a VC to get in.

But there are a handful of publicly traded vehicles that already hold stakes in these companies. You can buy them in any brokerage account, right now.

I dug into three options — an ETF and two closed-end funds from BlackRock — to figure out which one actually gives you meaningful exposure without getting fleeced on fees.

---

## The Three Options

### 1. AGIX — KraneShares AI & Technology ETF

**Structure:** Open-end ETF (trades like a stock, no discount/premium issues)

**Private Holdings:**
- **SpaceX/xAI** — 3.20% (post-merger, includes Grok/xAI exposure)
- **Anthropic** — 2.48% (direct cap table position, no SPV intermediary)
- Total private allocation: ~6%

**The rest (~94%):** Standard public AI/tech names — NVIDIA (5.3%), Microsoft (4.2%), Meta (3.9%), Alphabet (3.4%), Broadcom (3.2%), Apple (3.2%), TSMC (3.0%), Amazon (2.7%), Tesla (2.3%), Palantir (2.1%)

**Key stats:**
- AUM: $194M
- Expense ratio: **0.75%** (cheapest option)
- Yield: Minimal (<1%)
- Inception: 2024

**What makes it interesting:** AGIX holds Anthropic directly on the cap table — not through an SPV. That means cleaner price discovery and fewer hidden fees. The Anthropic position has reportedly **4x'd** since initial purchase (~10 months ago).

**The catch:** Only 6% private exposure. You're mostly buying a tech ETF that overlaps heavily with what many investors already own through index funds or individual positions.

---

### 2. BSTZ — BlackRock Science & Technology Term Trust

**Structure:** Closed-end term trust (trades on exchange, but at a discount/premium to NAV)

**Private Holdings (the crown jewels):**
- **Databricks** — **17.28%** (massive conviction bet)
- **ByteDance** — 2.38% (TikTok parent)
- **Anthropic** — 2.19%
- Total private allocation: **~22%**

**Key stats:**
- AUM: ~$2.7B
- Expense ratio: **1.48%** (most expensive)
- Yield: **~8.6%** (monthly distributions)
- Discount to NAV: **-10.9%** (buying $1 of assets for ~$0.89)
- **Liquidation date: June 2031** (extendable up to 18 months, or convertible to perpetual)

**What makes it interesting:** This is the **private AI king**. At 22% private allocation, you're getting meaningful exposure to three of the biggest pre-IPO AI companies in one vehicle. The 12% discount to NAV means you're buying those private stakes at a built-in markdown. And you're earning 8.6% yield while you wait for IPOs.

Databricks at 17% of the fund is a massive position. If Databricks IPOs at $80B+ (current private valuation is ~$62B), the NAV jump plus discount narrowing could be a powerful double catalyst.

**The catches:**
1. **1.48% expense ratio** — roughly 100x what you'd pay for an S&P 500 index fund
2. **2031 liquidation** — if markets are down when the trust dissolves, you take the hit. Though this could also be a feature: it forces a liquidity event rather than holding forever at a discount.
3. **Databricks concentration** — one private company is nearly 1/5 of the fund. High conviction, high risk.
4. **Distributions may include return of capital** — not all of that 8.6% yield is income. Some may be giving you your own money back. Check the 19a notices.

### 3. BST — BlackRock Science & Technology Trust

**Structure:** Closed-end perpetual fund (no liquidation date)

**Key stats:**
- AUM: ~$1.3B
- Expense ratio: **~1.07%**
- Yield: **~7.2%**
- Discount to NAV: **-5.1%**
- Private allocation: **~5%**

**What makes it interesting:** Same BlackRock management team as BSTZ, no liquidation deadline, decent yield. It's the "safer" version.

**The catch:** Only ~5% private exposure. At that level, you're essentially buying a tech income fund, not a private AI play. Most of the portfolio is public tech you can own directly for a fraction of the cost.

## The Fee Reality Check

These aren't cheap:

| Fund | Expense Ratio | Annual Cost on $50K |
|------|--------------|-------------------|
| **FXAIX** (S&P 500 for comparison) | 0.015% | $7.50 |
| **AGIX** | 0.75% | $375 |
| **BST** | 1.07% | $535 |
| **BSTZ** | 1.48% | $740 |

You're paying 50-100x more than a basic index fund. The question is whether access to Databricks, Anthropic, and ByteDance at pre-IPO prices is worth it. If even one of these companies IPOs at a premium to current private valuations, the fee becomes noise.

For BSTZ specifically: 1.48% fee against an 8.6% distribution yield means you're still netting ~7.1% income while waiting. And you're buying at a 12% discount. The math works if the private holdings perform.

## The Bigger Picture

We're in a unique window. The biggest AI companies are still private, but some are likely to IPO within 12-24 months:

- **Databricks** — Reportedly exploring IPO for late 2026 or 2027
- **Anthropic** — Could IPO or do another mega-round in 2026-2027
- **ByteDance** — TikTok resolution could force an IPO of parts of the business
- **SpaceX** — Starlink IPO has been rumored for years

Once these companies go public, the "access premium" disappears and everyone can buy them directly. The arbitrage opportunity exists *now* — particularly in BSTZ where you're buying at a 12% discount to a NAV that includes these pre-IPO valuations.

Whether the premium is worth it depends on your conviction that these companies are as valuable as their private market valuations suggest. Given the AI buildout we're living through, I'd argue the case is strong.
