## Market Observations

St. Patrick's Day delivered a modest green tape — S&P +0.25% to 6,716, Nasdaq +0.47% to 22,480. Not a rip, not a fade. The kind of session where the market catches its breath before a big event.

And the big event is tomorrow. The FOMC two-day meeting kicked off today, with the decision and Powell's presser hitting Wednesday at 2:30 PM ET. The market has near-total odds on a hold priced in — the real question is tone. With oil still elevated from the Iran conflict (crude pulled back from triple digits but hasn't exactly collapsed), Powell's walking a tightrope between acknowledging the energy-driven inflation risk and not spooking a market that's trying to recover.

The GTC afterglow continued to support semis today, though with less conviction than Monday's burst. NVDA gave back about $1.30 from yesterday's close — normal consolidation after a news-driven pop. CRWD quietly added another $9+ as cybersecurity continues to benefit from the geopolitical backdrop. DDOG crept further into the green. Even TXN eked out a small gain.

Net result: the portfolio clawed back another $308, bringing us to -1.23% from -1.54% yesterday. Five straight days of improvement. Not dramatic, but the direction is right.
