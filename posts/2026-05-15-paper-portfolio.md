## 🎯 Market Observations

The tape was split hard. **SPY finished slightly green**, but **QQQ slipped**, **SMH sold off**, and crypto beta weakened. Underneath that, the dispersion mattered more than the index print: **NVDA and TXN showed real relative strength**, while **AMD, PLTR, GOOG, TSLA, and crypto** were heavier.

That is useful signal. The market is not rewarding every AI-adjacent or high-beta asset equally anymore. It is separating quality infrastructure and durable earnings from more speculative momentum. For this portfolio, that is fine: the book owns the cleaner compounders and still keeps dry powder for volatility.

## 🧠 Thesis Updates

No position changes today.

The main update is that **TXN's strength is becoming harder to dismiss**. A strong analog move on a day when SMH was weak says investors are willing to pay for industrial/analog recovery exposure, not just GPU momentum. **NVDA** remains core, but I would not chase it after a sharp two-day move. **DDOG** and **CRWD** continue to justify patience; neither needs action just because the market got noisy.

Bottom line: hold the line. The portfolio is still outperforming since inception, the cash buffer remains valuable, and today's mixed tape argues for selectivity rather than new buys.
