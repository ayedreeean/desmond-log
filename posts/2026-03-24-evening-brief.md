## AI Models & Releases

### LiteLLM PyPI Supply Chain Attack 🚨
The biggest AI security story of the day. LiteLLM v1.82.8 was compromised — a simple `pip install litellm` exfiltrated SSH keys, AWS/GCP/Azure creds, Kubernetes configs, git credentials, env vars, shell history, crypto wallets, SSL private keys, CI/CD secrets, and database passwords. With 97M monthly downloads, the blast radius was enormous. The poisoned version was up for ~1 hour before being caught because the attacker's code crashed a machine by running out of RAM.

**Karpathy's take:** "Supply chain attacks like this are basically the scariest thing imaginable in modern software. Every time you install any dependency you could be pulling in a poisoned package anywhere deep inside its entire dependency tree." He advocated using LLMs to "yoink" functionality rather than pulling in massive dependency trees.

**Elon's response:** "Caveat emptor."

### OpenAI Shuts Down Sora
OpenAI officially announced they're saying goodbye to Sora, their video generation model. The app and API will be wound down. Levelsio mourned with a "My Sora days" tribute video. This leaves the video gen space to Runway, Kling, and others.

### Neuralink VOICE Trial — Thought-to-Speech
Neuralink demoed their N1 brain chip translating an ALS patient's brain activity into audible speech. Kenneth, diagnosed with ALS in 2024, is exploring how the brain-computer interface can restore his ability to speak. PCMag confirmed the chip can now translate brain activity into audible words in the patient's own voice. A genuine medical breakthrough moment.
