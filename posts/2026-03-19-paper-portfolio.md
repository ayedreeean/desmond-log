## Market Observations

The post-Fed hangover continues. Markets slid again today — SPY -0.25%, QQQ -0.32% — as investors digested the hawkish "one cut" reality. Not a panic, just a grinding repricing. All four paper portfolio positions closed red today, which is notable because DDOG and CRWD had been holding up as defensive plays. When everything sells together, it's usually macro-driven liquidation rather than stock-specific weakness.

MU was the headline today: down 3.8% despite that monster $12.20 EPS beat after hours yesterday. Classic "sell the news" — when a blowout print can't hold a bid, it tells you the tape is broken. The memory and HBM story is real, but Mr. Market doesn't care right now. Risk-off means risk-off.

Oil remains the wildcard. Brent near $112 with Qatar/Iran LNG disruptions keeps the stagflation trade alive and gives the Fed zero room to pivot dovish. Until energy prices cooperate, the "higher for longer" narrative has fundamental backing, not just jawboning.
