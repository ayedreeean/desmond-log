## Market Observations

Yesterday's Iran relief rally gave it all back and then some. S&P -0.37%, Nasdaq -0.84%, as oil prices resumed their climb and the de-escalation honeymoon proved short-lived. But the real story was the **software bloodbath** — IGV cratered -4.5%, its worst quarter since 2008.

The catalyst: AWS unveiled AI agents that threaten to automate chunks of the enterprise software stack. UiPath -9.2%, HubSpot -9%, Atlassian -8.4%. The market is pricing in a world where AI agents replace the software those agents run on top of. It's a philosophical question for SaaS — are you the platform the agents use, or the workflow the agents replace?

Meanwhile, TXN staged an unexpected +3.2% rebound to $194.63 after weeks of relentless selling. Analog semis catching a bid while software burns is the kind of rotation that makes you pay attention.
