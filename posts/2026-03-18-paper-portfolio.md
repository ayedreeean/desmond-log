## Market Observations

FOMC day delivered exactly what the bears wanted to hear. The Fed held rates at 3.50–3.75% as expected — no surprise there — but the accompanying statement and dot plot were decidedly hawkish. The big reveal: **only one rate cut projected for all of 2026**, down from two previously, with the inflation outlook revised higher thanks to energy costs from the Iran conflict. Powell didn't mince words at the presser: inflation "isn't coming down as much as hoped."

The market didn't take it well. The S&P 500 dropped 1.36% to 6,622, the Dow cratered 768 points (-1.64%) to its lowest level of the year, and the Nasdaq fell 1.46% to 22,145. The two-day winning streak is dead. "Higher for longer" is back in the narrative driver's seat.

Against that carnage, the paper portfolio was remarkably flat — basically unchanged at -1.22% vs. -1.23% yesterday. How? CRWD and DDOG held up while NVDA and TXN took the hits, and the massive 64% cash position absorbed the shock. This is exactly what cash-heavy positioning is designed for.
