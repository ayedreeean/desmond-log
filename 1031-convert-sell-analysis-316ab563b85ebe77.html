❌ This Strategy Doesn't Work

Congress closed this loophole in 2008 with the Housing Assistance Tax Act.

⚖️ Two Laws Block This

🚫 Problem #1: The 5-Year Holding Rule (Section 121(d)(10)) If a property was acquired via 1031 exchange, you must own it for at least 5 years before the Section 121 exclusion applies. 2 years rental + 2 years primary = 4 years. Does not qualify.
🚫 Problem #2: Non-Qualified Use Proration (Section 121(b)(5)) Even if you hold for 5+ years, the exclusion is reduced proportionally for any time the property was not a primary residence. Renting it out counts as "non-qualified use."

✅ What Actually Works Better

🏆

Option A: Keep CA + Hire a Property Manager

Highest income (~$53K/yr), keep Prop 13, zero tax hit, fully reversible. Solves the family management problem for ~$550/mo. Stepped-up basis at inheritance eliminates all gains — same as the 1031 path but simpler.

🏡

Option B: 1031 to TX and Hold Until Inheritance

If lifestyle matters (closer to Ellen, one property, done with CA) — 1031 into TX and hold it. Don't try to convert and sell. The stepped-up basis at inheritance eliminates the deferred gains permanently. Income is ~$7K/yr less than CA + PM, but family proximity may be worth it.

📈

Option C: Sell Everything, Invest Proceeds

If they want zero landlord responsibilities forever. Pay the ~$550K tax, invest ~$1.05M in index funds. Lower starting income but highest long-term growth potential. Simplest possible life. Stepped-up basis still applies to the investment portfolio at inheritance.

💡 The Key Insight

Stepped-up basis at inheritance works regardless of which path you choose. Whether the properties are in CA, exchanged to TX, or sold and invested — when Ellen inherits, the cost basis resets to fair market value and all gains are eliminated. The 1031 "convert and sell" strategy tries to capture tax savings during their lifetime, but the costs of executing it ($266K+) exceed the tax savings ($158–200K). The math doesn't work.

The real decision isn't about taxes — it's about lifestyle. Want maximum income with minimum change? → Keep CA, hire a PM.
Want to be near Ellen and simplify? → 1031 to TX and hold forever.
Want out of real estate entirely? → Sell, pay the tax, invest.
